Overview

As a recent visitor to Key West via ferry, I needed transportation upon my arrival. Rental cars were expensive and it can be difficult to find parking on a crowded island. A moped seemed like a decent alternative given its cheap cost and easy maneuvering. But what were the safety considerations? After an internet search or two, I discovered that the Key West Police Department raised concerns about moped accidents in the past. So I decided to do some sleuthing on my own.

Background

The safety of different kind of vehicles are constantly being evaluated by researchers. And there are a variety of products available for rental or purchase. The possibilities include things like hover boards and scooters.[1]

Data and model

Florida has an open data portal. There, a dataset regarding motorcycle and moped “crashes” is maintained. The data were collected beginning in 2012 through 2019. The 2019 data are suspect in that crashes notably declined and its cause is unknown.

Crashes within Cities by Day

Data was available for the calendar years 2012 through 2019. The crashes were summed by date and faceted by year. Years 2012 through 2014 were omitted to expand the vertical plotting area and make the variation more prevalent. The number of crashes decreased beginning in 2019.

Crashes Key West vs. Florida Cities

There was significant variation in the number of motorcyle/moped crashes recorded in Key West. In 2017, there were only six crashes and, in 2019, there were only nine. It’s also important to remember that the statewide data represents the rate per 100 thousand residents that live in cities in Florida. Since cities have greater population densities and more traffic, the crash incidence is likely higher than if it was computed with more rural residents included.
